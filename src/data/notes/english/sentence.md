# Sentence Correction/Rearrangement | वाक्य सुधार/पुनर्रचना

- **Subject-Verb Agreement**: Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs. Watch for collective nouns and intervening phrases.

- **Tense Consistency**: Maintain tense consistency within a sentence/passage unless time shift is intentional. Past, Present, Future should align logically.

- **Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement**: Pronouns must match their nouns in number and gender. Avoid ambiguous pronoun references.

- **Parallel Structure**: Items in lists/comparisons must have identical grammatical form (noun-noun, verb-verb, clause-clause).

- **Article Usage**: "A/An" for singular indefinite; "The" for definite. Omit articles for plural generics. Non-countables rarely use articles.

- **Word Order/Misplaced Modifiers**: Place modifiers adjacent to words they modify. Dangling participles create ambiguity ("Walking down the street, the building appeared").

- **Preposition Errors**: Common mistakes—"different from" (not "to"), "blame on" vs "blame for", "depend on" (not "of").

- **Sentence Rearrangement Logic**: Identify topic sentence (usually first), then follow chronological/logical flow. Check cohesive devices (this, that, therefore, however).

- **Active vs. Passive Voice**: Prefer active voice (agent-action-object). Passive weakens meaning unless emphasis is required.

- **Comparative/Superlative Forms**: Use comparative (-er/more) for two items; superlative (-est/most) for three+. Use "as...as" for equality.

- **Common Errors**: Avoid double negatives, double comparatives, wrong verb forms ("should do" not "should done"), and run-on sentences.

- **Connectors & Transitions**: Conjunctions (and, but, or) link similar ideas; transitional phrases (moreover, however, therefore) show relationships between sentences and maintain paragraph coherence.
