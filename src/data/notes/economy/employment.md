# रोजगार व ग्रामीण रोजगार योजना / Employment & Rural Employment Schemes

Evolution of India's rural wage-employment schemes, in order — a favourite MPSC theme (the shift from discretionary schemes to a rights-based legal guarantee).

## कालक्रम / Timeline (lakshात ठेवा)

- **NREP — National Rural Employment Programme (1980):** राष्ट्रीय ग्रामीण रोजगार कार्यक्रम. Started in 1980 to give wage employment in rural areas and build durable community assets.
- **RLEGP — Rural Landless Employment Guarantee Programme (1983):** ग्रामीण भूमिहीन रोजगार हमी कार्यक्रम. Launched in 1983, aimed specifically at the **landless (भूमिहीन)** — employment for at least one member of each landless rural household.
- **JRY — Jawahar Rozgar Yojana (1989):** जवाहर रोजगार योजना. In 1989 **NREP and RLEGP were merged into JRY** (NREP + RLEGP → JRY).
- **EAS — Employment Assurance Scheme (1993):** रोजगार आश्वासन योजना. Started in 1993 to assure wage employment, mainly in the lean agricultural season.
- **JGSY — Jawahar Gram Samriddhi Yojana (1999):** जवाहर ग्राम समृद्धी योजना. In 1999 JRY was restructured/renamed as JGSY, focused on rural infrastructure and village development.
- **SGRY — Sampoorna Grameen Rozgar Yojana (2001):** संपूर्ण ग्रामीण रोजगार योजना. Formed in 2001 by **merging EAS and JGSY** (EAS + JGSY → SGRY).
- **NFFWP — National Food for Work Programme (2004):** राष्ट्रीय कामासाठी अन्न कार्यक्रम. Launched in 2004 in the most backward districts; part of the wages was paid in **foodgrains**.
- **NREGA — National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (2005):** राष्ट्रीय ग्रामीण रोजगार हमी कायदा. Passed in 2005 — a **legal guarantee** of up to **100 days** of wage employment per year to every rural household whose adults want unskilled manual work. Later renamed **MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA)**.

## लक्षात ठेवण्याचे mergers / Key merger facts

- **NREP + RLEGP = JRY** (1989)
- **EAS + JGSY = SGRY** (2001)
- NFFWP and SGRY were later subsumed into **NREGA / MGNREGA**.

## Why this chain matters

The big idea to remember: earlier schemes (NREP, RLEGP, JRY, EAS, JGSY, SGRY, NFFWP) were **government programmes** that could be started or stopped by policy. **NREGA/MGNREGA (2005)** was different — it made rural wage employment a **legal right** (a guaranteed number of days), which is the key conceptual jump MPSC likes to test.
