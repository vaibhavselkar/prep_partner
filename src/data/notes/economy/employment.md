# रोजगार व ग्रामीण रोजगार योजना / Employment & Rural Employment Schemes

Evolution of India's rural wage-employment schemes, in order — a favourite MPSC theme (the shift from discretionary schemes to a rights-based legal guarantee).

## कामगार कोण? / Who is a worker?

- राष्ट्रीय उत्पन्नात भर घालणाऱ्या कोणत्याही आर्थिक क्रियेत गुंतलेली व्यक्ती "कामगार" (worker) मानली जाते / Anyone engaged in an economic activity that adds to national income counts as a worker — whether paid by an employer or self-employed.
- आजारपण, सण, हवामान यामुळे तात्पुरते काम बंद असणारेही कामगारच धरले जातात / Even those temporarily away from work (illness, festivals, bad weather) are still counted as workers.
- घरातील विनावेतन काम (उदा. गृहिणींचे घरकाम) सध्याच्या व्याख्येत कामगार धरले जात नाही — ही एक मर्यादा मानली जाते / Unpaid domestic/household work is NOT counted as economic activity under the current definition — economists flag this as an undercounting problem, especially for women.

## कामगारांचे प्रकार (दर्जानुसार) / Workers by status

- **स्वयंरोजगार (Self-employed):** स्वतःचा व्यवसाय चालवणारे — भारतातील सर्वाधिक मोठा वर्ग / Own and run their own enterprise; the single largest category of India's workforce.
- **नियमित वेतनी कर्मचारी (Regular salaried employees):** एखाद्या नियोक्त्याकडून नियमित वेतन मिळणारे / Engaged by an employer and paid wages on a regular basis.
- **आकस्मिक वेतनी मजूर (Casual wage labourers):** रोजंदारीवर, अनेकदा एकापेक्षा जास्त नियोक्त्यांकडे काम करणारे — सर्वात असुरक्षित वर्ग / Work on a day-to-day/casual basis, often for more than one employer; considered the most vulnerable of the three categories.
- ग्रामीण भागात स्वयंरोजगाराचे प्रमाण अधिक (स्वतःची शेती), तर शहरी भागात नियमित वेतनी नोकऱ्यांचे प्रमाण अधिक / Self-employment dominates in rural areas (own farming); regular salaried work is relatively more common in urban areas.

## कामगार-लोकसंख्या गुणोत्तर / Worker-population ratio & participation

- **कामगार-लोकसंख्या गुणोत्तर (Worker-population ratio):** एकूण लोकसंख्येपैकी प्रत्यक्ष आर्थिक क्रियेत गुंतलेल्यांचे प्रमाण / The share of total population actively engaged in economic activity — a key indicator of how many people are contributing to production.
- स्त्रियांच्या तुलनेत पुरुषांचे सहभाग प्रमाण खूप जास्त आहे; शहरी स्त्रियांचे सहभाग प्रमाण ग्रामीण स्त्रियांपेक्षाही कमी / Male participation is much higher than female; urban women's participation is lower than rural women's.
- घरगुती व शेतीवरील विनावेतन कामामुळे स्त्री-कामगारांची वास्तविक संख्या कमी मोजली जाते असे अर्थतज्ज्ञांचे मत / Economists argue unpaid household and farm work by women leads to underestimation of the actual number of women workers.

## अनौपचारिकीकरण / Formal vs informal sector & informalisation

- **औपचारिक (संघटित) क्षेत्र (Formal/organised sector):** सर्व सरकारी आस्थापने + 10 किंवा अधिक कामगार असलेली खासगी आस्थापने — येथे सामाजिक सुरक्षा (भविष्य निर्वाह निधी, निवृत्तिवेतन इ.) मिळते / All public-sector establishments plus private establishments employing 10+ hired workers; workers here get social-security benefits and better job protection.
- **अनौपचारिक (असंघटित) क्षेत्र (Informal/unorganised sector):** लहान आस्थापने, स्वयंरोजगारित व्यक्ती, बहुतांश आकस्मिक मजूर — नियमित उत्पन्न किंवा सामाजिक सुरक्षा नाही / Small enterprises, most self-employed persons and casual labourers who work for multiple employers; no regular income or social-security cover.
- भारतातील बहुसंख्य कामगार (सुमारे नऊ-दशांश) अनौपचारिक क्षेत्रात आहेत — विकासाबरोबर हे प्रमाण कमी होईल ही अपेक्षा प्रत्यक्षात फोल ठरली / The vast majority (roughly nine-tenths) of India's workforce is informal — contrary to the expectation that the formal sector's share would rise as the economy grew.
- **अनौपचारिकीकरण (Informalisation of workforce):** औपचारिक क्षेत्रातील नोकऱ्या न वाढता, नवीन रोजगार मुख्यतः अनौपचारिक/असुरक्षित स्वरूपाचा निर्माण होण्याची प्रक्रिया (उदा. अहमदाबादच्या कापड गिरण्या बंद पडल्यावर हजारो कामगार असंघटित क्षेत्रात ढकलले गेले) / The process by which new employment is increasingly informal/insecure rather than formal — illustrated by Ahmedabad's textile-mill closures, which pushed tens of thousands of permanent workers into the informal sector.

## बेरोजगारीचे प्रकार / Types of unemployment

- **खुली बेरोजगारी (Open unemployment):** काम शोधणारी पण अजिबात काम न मिळणारी व्यक्ती / Person actively seeking work but unable to find any work at all.
- **छुपी/प्रच्छन्न बेरोजगारी (Disguised unemployment):** शेतजमिनीवर प्रत्यक्ष गरजेपेक्षा जास्त लोक (उदा. कुटुंबातील सदस्य) राबत असतात — त्यांना काढले तरी उत्पादनात घट होत नाही / More people (often family members) work on a farm than the work actually requires; removing the "extra" workers would not reduce output — common on Indian farms.
- **हंगामी बेरोजगारी (Seasonal unemployment):** शेतीकाम हंगामी असल्याने वर्षातील काही महिने काम उपलब्ध नसणे (उदा. पावसाळ्यात मजूर शहराकडून गावाकडे परततात) / Work is unavailable for part of the year because agricultural work itself is seasonal — a common pattern in rural India.

## रोजगारविरहित वाढ व कामाचे आकस्मिकीकरण / Jobless growth & casualisation

- **रोजगारविरहित वाढ (Jobless growth):** सकल देशांतर्गत उत्पादन (GDP) वाढत असतानाही रोजगार वाढीचा दर त्या तुलनेत खूपच कमी राहणे / GDP grows steadily but employment grows much more slowly — output rises without a matching rise in jobs; the gap widened noticeably from the late 1990s.
- **कामाचे आकस्मिकीकरण (Casualisation of workforce):** स्वयंरोजगार व नियमित वेतनी नोकऱ्यांकडून आकस्मिक/रोजंदारी कामाकडे कामगारांचे स्थलांतर होण्याची प्रक्रिया — यामुळे कामगारांची असुरक्षितता वाढते / The shift of workers away from self-employment and regular salaried jobs toward casual wage work, which increases worker vulnerability (less job security, no fixed benefits).
- संरचनात्मक बदल: प्राथमिक (कृषी) क्षेत्रातील रोजगाराचा वाटा कालांतराने घटत असून, द्वितीय व सेवा क्षेत्रांचा वाटा वाढत आहे, तरी कृषी क्षेत्र आजही सर्वात मोठा रोजगारदाता आहे / Structurally, agriculture's share of employment has declined over the decades while industry and services have grown, yet agriculture still employs the largest single share of India's workforce.

## कालक्रम / Timeline (lakshात ठेवा)

- **NREP — National Rural Employment Programme (1980):** राष्ट्रीय ग्रामीण रोजगार कार्यक्रम. Started in 1980 to give wage employment in rural areas and build durable community assets.
- **RLEGP — Rural Landless Employment Guarantee Programme (1983):** ग्रामीण भूमिहीन रोजगार हमी कार्यक्रम. Launched in 1983, aimed specifically at the **landless (भूमिहीन)** — employment for at least one member of each landless rural household.
- **JRY — Jawahar Rozgar Yojana (1989):** जवाहर रोजगार योजना. In 1989 **NREP and RLEGP were merged into JRY** (NREP + RLEGP → JRY).
- **EAS — Employment Assurance Scheme (1993):** रोजगार आश्वासन योजना. Started in 1993 to assure wage employment, mainly in the lean agricultural season.
- **JGSY — Jawahar Gram Samriddhi Yojana (1999):** जवाहर ग्राम समृद्धी योजना. In 1999 JRY was restructured/renamed as JGSY, focused on rural infrastructure and village development.
- **SGRY — Sampoorna Grameen Rozgar Yojana (2001):** संपूर्ण ग्रामीण रोजगार योजना. Formed in 2001 by **merging EAS and JGSY** (EAS + JGSY → SGRY).
- **NFFWP — National Food for Work Programme (2004):** राष्ट्रीय कामासाठी अन्न कार्यक्रम. Launched in 2004 in the most backward districts; part of the wages was paid in **foodgrains**.
- **NREGA — National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (2005):** राष्ट्रीय ग्रामीण रोजगार हमी कायदा. Passed in 2005 — a **legal guarantee** of up to **100 days** of wage employment per year to every rural household whose adults want unskilled manual work. Later renamed **MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA)**.

## लक्षात ठेवण्याचे mergers / Key merger facts

- **NREP + RLEGP = JRY** (1989)
- **EAS + JGSY = SGRY** (2001)
- NFFWP and SGRY were later subsumed into **NREGA / MGNREGA**.

## Why this chain matters

The big idea to remember: earlier schemes (NREP, RLEGP, JRY, EAS, JGSY, SGRY, NFFWP) were **government programmes** that could be started or stopped by policy. **NREGA/MGNREGA (2005)** was different — it made rural wage employment a **legal right** (a guaranteed number of days), which is the key conceptual jump MPSC likes to test.
